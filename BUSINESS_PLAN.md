   - Weaknesses: Generic feel, no handmade aspect

3. **Wayfair, Overstock**
   - Strengths: Huge inventory, competitive pricing
   - Weaknesses: No handmade, quality inconsistent
   - Market Position: Online furniture/decor leaders
   - Our Advantage: Artisan quality, sustainability focus

## Operations Plan

### Production Process

#### Workflow Overview

**Product Type 1: Handmade Ceramics (Vases, Planters)**

1. **Design Phase**
   - Sketch new designs
   - Test proportions and glazes
   - Photograph samples

2. **Production Schedule**
   - Batch production: Every 2 weeks
   - Batch size: 12-18 pieces
   - Clay preparation: Day 1
   - Throwing/building: Days 1-2
   - Drying: Days 3-5
   - Bisque firing: Day 6
   - Glazing: Days 7-8
   - Glaze firing: Day 9
   - Quality check: Day 10
   - Photography: Day 11
   - Listing: Day 12
   - Ready to ship: Day 12-14

3. **Made-to-Order Process**
   - Customer orders custom color
   - Add to next production batch
   - Communicate 2-3 week timeline
   - Update customer on progress

### Detailed Risk Analysis & Mitigation

#### 1. Slow Initial Sales

**Risk**: Not achieving sales targets in first 6 months

**Likelihood**: High (60%)

**Impact**: Cash flow issues, difficulty reaching break-even

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Launch with pre-orders and waitlist
- Aggressive marketing in first 90 days
- Offer launch discount (15-20%) to drive initial sales
- Partner with 3-5 influencers before launch
- Have 3 months working capital reserved
- Consider part-time job income in first 6 months
- Test products with focus group before launch
- Start selling on Etsy before Shopify (faster sales)

**Contingency Plan**:
- Reduce operating expenses (cut marketing if needed)
- Extend timeline to profitability
- Take on contract/freelance work for income
- Scale back product line to focus on best sellers

---

#### 2. Supply Chain Disruption

**Risk**: Unable to source materials or products on time

**Likelihood**: Medium (40%)

**Impact**: Delayed orders, unhappy customers, lost sales

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Maintain relationships with 2 suppliers per category
- Keep 4-6 weeks of raw materials on hand
- Build in 2-week buffer for lead times
- Communicate proactively with customers if delays
- Have backup international suppliers
- Consider local suppliers to reduce shipping risks
- Diversify product line (not all dependent on one supplier)

**Contingency Plan**:
- Temporarily pause new orders if materials unavailable
- Offer substitutions with customer approval
- Refund orders if cannot fulfill in reasonable time
- Pivot to products with available materials

---

#### 3. Platform Policy Changes

**Risk**: Etsy, Shopify, or Instagram changes policies or fees

**Likelihood**: Medium (50%)

**Impact**: Increased costs, reduced visibility, policy violations

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Diversify across multiple platforms
- Build email list (owned audience)
- Transition to Shopify as primary (more control)
- Stay updated on platform policies
- Join seller communities for early warnings
- Read all policy update emails
- Never rely on single platform for >60% of sales

**Contingency Plan**:
- Quickly adapt to new policies
- Appeal any policy violations
- Shift marketing to other platforms
- Drive traffic directly to owned website
- Consider alternative platforms (Amazon Handmade, etc.)

---

#### 4. Increased Competition

**Risk**: More sellers entering handmade decor space

**Likelihood**: High (70%)

**Impact**: Price pressure, reduced margins, need for differentiation

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Focus on unique designs and quality
- Build strong brand identity
- Develop loyal customer base
- Offer personalization (harder to copy)
- Emphasize sustainability and story
- Provide excellent customer service
- Continuously innovate product line
- Protect IP (trademark, copyrights)

**Contingency Plan**:
- Emphasize brand differentiation in marketing
- Add exclusive products and limited editions
- Consider niche specialization
- Build community around brand
- Partner with influencers for credibility

---

#### 5. Product Quality Issues

**Risk**: Defects, customer complaints, negative reviews

**Likelihood**: Low (20%)

**Impact**: Reputation damage, returns, lost sales

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Implement strict quality control process
- Inspect every product before shipping
- Use high-quality materials
- Test production processes thoroughly
- Have clear return/replacement policy
- Address issues immediately and generously
- Request feedback from early customers
- Continuous improvement based on feedback

**Contingency Plan**:
- Offer immediate replacement or refund
- Cover return shipping for defects
- Send additional product as apology
- Learn from issues and fix root cause
- Document all QC failures to identify patterns

---

#### 6. Cash Flow Problems

**Risk**: Running out of cash before reaching profitability

**Likelihood**: Medium (40%)

**Impact**: Unable to fulfill orders, business closure

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Start with sufficient working capital (3+ months)
- Monitor cash flow weekly
- Maintain cash reserve (minimum $3,000)
- Negotiate payment terms with suppliers
- Manage inventory carefully (avoid over-ordering)
- Consider deposit for custom orders
- Use POD to minimize upfront inventory
- Control expenses rigorously in first year

**Contingency Plan**:
- Reduce expenses immediately (cut marketing)
- Liquidate excess inventory with sales
- Delay non-essential purchases
- Take on additional income source temporarily
- Negotiate extended payment terms
- Consider small business line of credit

---

#### 7. Economic Downturn

**Risk**: Recession reducing consumer spending on home decor

**Likelihood**: Low-Medium (30%)

**Impact**: Reduced sales, pressure on pricing

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Maintain lean operations (low fixed costs)
- Offer products at multiple price points
- Emphasize value and quality
- Market products as affordable luxury
- Diversify income (wholesale, subscriptions)
- Build emergency fund (6 months expenses)
- Focus on gifts (less affected by downturn)
- Emphasize durability and sustainability

**Contingency Plan**:
- Adjust product mix to lower price points
- Increase promotions and discounts
- Focus on existing customer retention
- Reduce operating expenses
- Consider corporate/B2B sales
- Offer payment plans for larger purchases

---

#### 8. Key Person Dependency (Owner)

**Risk**: Business relies entirely on owner; illness/burnout affects operations

**Likelihood**: High (60%)

**Impact**: Production stops, orders delayed, business at risk

**Mitigation Strategies**:
- Document all processes and procedures
- Cross-train part-time help by Year 2
- Use POD and curated products (less hands-on)
- Automate as much as possible (email, fulfillment)
- Build in personal time off
- Maintain work-life balance to prevent burnout
- Consider business interruption insurance
- Develop succession plan by Year 3

**Contingency Plan**:
- Pause new orders temporarily
- Communicate with customers proactively
- Hire emergency contract help
- Activate backup suppliers/fulfillment
- Have family member who can step in
- Maintain insurance for emergencies
