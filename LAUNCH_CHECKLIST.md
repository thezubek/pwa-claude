# Complete Launch Checklist - Home Decor Business

**Last Updated:** November 18, 2025

This comprehensive checklist will guide you through launching your home decor business from conception to your first sales and beyond.

---

## Pre-Launch Phase (8-12 Weeks Before Launch)

### Legal & Administrative (Weeks 1-2)

#### Business Formation
- [ ] Choose business name
- [ ] Check domain name availability (.com preferred)
- [ ] Check social media handle availability (@yourbusiness)
- [ ] Register business entity (LLC recommended)
- [ ] Obtain EIN from IRS (free at irs.gov)
- [ ] Open business bank account
- [ ] Apply for business credit card (optional but recommended)
- [ ] Register for sales tax permit in your state
- [ ] Get business insurance (general liability + product liability)

**Resources:**
- Business registration: Your state's Secretary of State website
- EIN: irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
- SCORE.org for free mentorship

#### Legal Documents
- [ ] Consult with attorney (one-time consultation)
- [ ] Create Terms of Service
- [ ] Create Privacy Policy
- [ ] Create Return/Refund Policy
- [ ] Create Shipping Policy
- [ ] Draft customer usage rights agreement (for custom uploads)
- [ ] Review intellectual property requirements

**Cost:** $300-500 for LLC, $500 for attorney consultation

### Product Development (Weeks 3-6)

#### POD Partner Setup
- [ ] Create Printful account
- [ ] Create Printify account (backup)
- [ ] Explore Gooten for specialty items
- [ ] Compare pricing and quality across partners
- [ ] Review shipping times and costs
- [ ] Understand production processes
- [ ] Read reviews and policies

#### Product Selection & Testing
- [ ] Choose initial 15 products from product catalog
- [ ] Create sample designs for each product
- [ ] Order 2-3 samples of each product (budget: $600)
- [ ] Test quality:
  - Print quality
  - Material quality
  - Sizing accuracy
  - Packaging
  - Shipping time
- [ ] Wash/durability test textiles
- [ ] Take detailed notes on each product
- [ ] Select final products for launch
- [ ] Make adjustments based on samples

**Cost:** $600 for samples

#### Photography
- [ ] Source or purchase photography equipment:
  - Camera or smartphone with good camera
  - Tripod
  - Lighting (ring light or softbox) - $50-100
  - Backdrop (white foam board or seamless paper) - $20-40
- [ ] Plan product photoshoot
- [ ] Create shot list (5+ photos per product)
- [ ] Source props (plants, books, coffee mugs, neutral decor)
- [ ] Scout home locations for lifestyle shots
- [ ] Conduct photoshoot (dedicate 1-2 full days)
- [ ] Edit all photos for consistency
- [ ] Organize photo library
- [ ] Create product mockups if needed

**Cost:** $150-400 for photography equipment and props

## When Things Don't Go As Planned

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Low Sales in First Month**
- Review marketing strategy
- Improve product photos/descriptions
- Increase marketing budget
- Offer limited-time promotion
- Request feedback from non-buyers
- Test different products
- Don't panic - growth takes time!

**Technical Website Issues**
- Contact Shopify support (24/7)
- Check app conflicts
- Test in incognito mode
- Review recent changes
- Hire Shopify expert if needed

**Negative Reviews/Feedback**
- Respond professionally and promptly
- Offer solutions and remedies
- Learn from legitimate criticism
- Improve based on feedback
- Don't take it personally

**Cash Flow Problems**
- Cut non-essential expenses
- Pause or reduce ad spend temporarily
- Run promotion to boost cash
- Consider small business loan
- Focus on high-margin products

**Burnout**
- Take breaks when needed
- Don't try to do everything at once
- Hire help (even part-time VA)
- Automate what you can
- Remember your "why"
