### Small-Batch Manufacturing Considerations

**Typical Batch Sizes:**
- Small batch: 50-100 items
- Medium batch: 100-400 items
- Some manufacturers: No more than 400 items = small batch

**Benefits of Small-Batch:**
- Lower upfront investment
- Test products before scaling
- Less inventory risk
- Flexibility to adjust designs
- Support local communities
- Artisan quality control
- Sustainability (less waste)

**Finding the Right Partner:**
- Define your product specifications clearly
- Get samples before committing
- Understand MOQs and lead times
- Build relationships (communication is key)
- Visit facilities when possible
- Start with smaller orders to test quality
- Negotiate payment terms

---

### Alternative Manufacturing Models

**1. In-House Production:**
- **Pros:** Full control, no MOQs, immediate adjustments
- **Cons:** Equipment costs, time-intensive, scalability limits
- **Best For:** Highly specialized or simple products

**2. Local Artisan Partnerships:**
- **Pros:** Support local, quality control, unique products
- **Cons:** Limited capacity, potentially higher costs
- **Best For:** Handmade, small-batch, local story

**3. Co-Packing/Contract Manufacturing:**
- **Pros:** Professional quality, scalability, less hands-on
- **Cons:** MOQs, less flexibility, requires upfront investment
- **Best For:** Scaling proven products

### Comparison: Faire vs. Etsy

| Feature | Faire | Etsy |
|---------|-------|------|
| Customer | Retailers (B2B) | Consumers (B2C) |
| Order Size | Larger wholesale orders | Individual purchases |
| Commission | 15-25% | ~10% total fees |
| Price Point | Wholesale pricing | Retail pricing |
| Volume | Higher per order | Lower per order |

## REGIONAL SUPPLIER CONSIDERATIONS

### Domestic (USA) vs. International Suppliers

**USA Suppliers:**
**Pros:**
- Faster shipping (days vs. weeks)
- Easier communication (time zones, language)
- Simpler logistics (no customs, duties)
- "Made in USA" marketing appeal
- Support local economy
- Easier to visit and inspect

**Cons:**
- Often higher costs
- May have higher MOQs
- Limited specialty options

**Best For:** Premium positioning, fast turnaround, Made in USA story

---

**International Suppliers:**
**Pros:**
- Lower costs (especially Asia)
- Access to specialty products
- Unique artisan traditions (e.g., Portuguese ceramics)
- Large-scale capabilities

**Cons:**
- Longer lead times (8-16 weeks)
- Shipping costs and complexity
- Import duties and customs
- Communication challenges
- Harder to verify quality and ethics
- Time zone differences

**Best For:** Cost-sensitive products, specialty items, scaling volume

---

**Hybrid Approach (Recommended):**
- USA: Time-sensitive, premium, "Made in USA" products
- International: Unique specialty items, cost-effective staples
- Diversify to reduce risk of single-source dependency

## PAYMENT TERMS GLOSSARY

**Common Payment Terms:**

- **Net 30/60/90:** Payment due 30/60/90 days after invoice date
- **2/10 Net 30:** 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise due in 30
- **50% Deposit:** Half paid upfront, half on delivery
- **Payment on Delivery (POD):** Pay when order arrives
- **Prepayment:** Full payment before production begins
- **Letter of Credit (LC):** Bank guarantees payment (international)

**For New Businesses:**
- Expect prepayment or 50% deposit initially
- Build to Net 30 terms as relationship develops
- Negotiate better terms as order volume increases
